NYC Speed
January 14th, 2025
Last fiscal year, around 3 million drivers sped through school zones in New York City, committing nearly 8 million violations. Most only did so once or twice, but a small group of drivers committed speeding violations many times over.

Of course at the most extreme ends of the spectrum, 0% of drivers account for 0% of speeding violations, and 100% of drivers together account for all 100% of speeding violations. But in between those extremes, there's a story about a small set of drivers who together account for an inordinately large share of violations.
Half of drivers sped through school zones at least twice last year. Together, they account for over 80% of speeding violations.
Even more extremely, the top 1% of drivers account for over a tenth of last year's speeding violations. Taken together, these ~30,000 drivers speed through school zones nearly 850,000 times over the course of a year. Each one sped at least 17 times.
This small group of repeat offenders drives mostly around Brooklyn and Queens, New York's two most poopulous boroughs, and boroughs with relatively light traffic.
